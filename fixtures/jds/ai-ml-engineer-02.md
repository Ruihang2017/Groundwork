# Machine Learning Engineer, Computer Vision — Fenwick Analytics

## About the role

Fenwick Analytics builds automated quality-inspection systems for industrial
manufacturing lines. Our cameras sit above conveyor belts and catch defects — cracks,
misprints, contamination — faster and more consistently than a human inspector can. We
are a Series B company of about 120 people, and our vision team of eight owns the models
that decide, in under 50 milliseconds, whether a part passes or fails.

We are looking for a mid-level machine learning engineer to own end-to-end computer
vision model development for new customer deployments, from labelling strategy through to
on-device inference.

## What you'll do

- Design, train, and deploy object-detection and segmentation models that run on
  edge hardware next to the production line.
- Build active-learning loops that surface the most informative images for our labelling
  team, keeping annotation cost down as we onboard new factories.
- Optimise models for latency and memory so they run on constrained edge GPUs (NVIDIA
  Jetson class), using quantisation and pruning where needed.
- Partner with field engineers to debug model drift when a customer changes lighting,
  camera angle, or product line.

## Requirements

- 3–5 years of industry experience building and shipping computer vision models to
  production, not just research prototypes.
- Deep hands-on skill with PyTorch and at least one modern detection or segmentation
  framework (Detectron2, YOLO family, or MMDetection).
- Strong grasp of the vision fundamentals: convolutional architectures, data
  augmentation, transfer learning, and the trade-offs between detection, segmentation,
  and classification.
- Demonstrated experience optimising models for edge or real-time inference —
  quantisation, ONNX/TensorRT export, or model distillation.
- Comfort owning the full data lifecycle: labelling guidelines, dataset versioning, and
  train/validation splits that reflect real production distributions.
- Solid Python engineering practices — you write tested, reviewable code, not one-off
  scripts.

## Nice to have

- Experience with manufacturing, robotics, or other industrial domains.
- Familiarity with MLOps tooling for continuous retraining and monitoring.
- C++ experience for performance-critical inference paths.

## Logistics

- Location: Austin, Texas. On-site four days per week — proximity to our hardware lab is
  important for this role, so it is not eligible for full remote.
- We sponsor and transfer H-1B visas and support green-card applications for engineers we
  hire.
- Full-time. Base salary USD 145,000–175,000 plus equity and a hardware budget.
- Occasional travel (up to 15%) to customer factory sites in the US and Mexico.

Fenwick Analytics is committed to building an inclusive team and welcomes applicants from
every background.
