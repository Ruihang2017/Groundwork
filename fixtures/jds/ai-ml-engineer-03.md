# Senior ML Engineer, MLOps & Infrastructure — Cadence Health

## About the role

Cadence Health operates a clinical-decision-support platform used by regional hospital
networks across the United States. Our models predict patient deterioration, flag
sepsis risk, and forecast bed demand. Because these predictions touch patient care, our
bar for reliability, reproducibility, and auditability is unusually high.

We are hiring a senior ML engineer to own the infrastructure that takes a model from a
researcher's notebook to a monitored, versioned, audit-logged production service. You
will not primarily be training models — you will be building the platform that lets a
team of fifteen data scientists ship them safely.

## What you'll do

- Own and evolve our ML platform: feature store, model registry, training orchestration,
  and the CI/CD that promotes a model from staging to production.
- Build monitoring for data drift, prediction drift, and model performance decay, with
  alerting that pages the on-call data scientist before a hospital notices.
- Design reproducible training pipelines so that any production model can be rebuilt bit
  for bit from a versioned dataset and config — a hard regulatory requirement in our
  domain.
- Mentor data scientists on production engineering practices and review their deployment
  pull requests.

## Requirements

- 6+ years of software or ML engineering experience, including at least 3 years building
  ML platforms or productionising models at scale.
- Strong software engineering background in Python; you have shipped and maintained
  production services, not just training scripts.
- Deep experience with at least one workflow orchestrator (Airflow, Kubeflow, Dagster,
  or Prefect) and one model-serving framework.
- Hands-on Kubernetes and cloud infrastructure experience (AWS preferred), including
  infrastructure-as-code with Terraform.
- Practical understanding of ML monitoring: what drift is, how to detect it, and how to
  respond to it in a regulated environment.
- Experience with reproducibility tooling — dataset versioning (DVC or similar), model
  registries (MLflow), and experiment tracking.

## Nice to have

- Prior work in healthcare, fintech, or another regulated, high-stakes domain.
- Familiarity with HIPAA and audit-logging requirements.
- Experience with real-time / streaming feature pipelines.

## Logistics

- Location: Remote within the United States. This role is fully remote; we have team
  members in twelve states and gather in person once a quarter.
- You must be authorised to work in the US. We do not sponsor visas for this position.
- Full-time. Base salary USD 185,000–215,000 plus equity, with an on-call rotation
  stipend.
- Because we operate in a regulated clinical environment, all hires complete a background
  check before start.

Cadence Health is an equal-opportunity employer and values a diversity of perspectives on
our engineering team.
